HISTORY

Earth formed about 4.54 billion years ago from the dust and gas of the early solar system, coalescing under gravity into a molten sphere that slowly cooled to form a solid crust.

Within its first billion years, liquid water gathered into oceans and the earliest single-celled life appeared — the beginning of an unbroken chain of living things that continues today.

Roughly 2.4 billion years ago, photosynthetic microbes flooded the atmosphere with oxygen in the Great Oxidation Event, reshaping the air and making complex life possible.

Earth is the only known world with plate tectonics, liquid surface water, and a living biosphere. Its single large Moon, likely born from a colossal early collision, steadies its tilt and gives us tides.
