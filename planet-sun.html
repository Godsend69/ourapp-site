HISTORY

The Sun was born about 4.6 billion years ago when a vast cloud of gas and dust collapsed under its own gravity, igniting nuclear fusion at its core and lighting the solar system into being.

It is a colossal sphere of hot plasma, fusing about 600 million tons of hydrogen into helium every second, releasing the light and warmth that make life on Earth possible.

Energy born in its core takes tens of thousands of years to struggle to the surface, then just eight minutes to cross space and reach us as sunlight.

The Sun holds 99.8% of all the mass in the solar system, and every planet, moon, comet, and speck of dust orbits it. In billions of years it will swell into a red giant, then fade into a white dwarf.
