HISTORY

Mercury is the smallest planet and the closest to the Sun, racing around it in just 88 days — the fastest orbit of any planet, earning its name from the swift Roman messenger.

Its surface is heavily cratered like the Moon, a frozen record of billions of years of impacts, with almost no atmosphere to wear them away.

It swings from about 430°C in sunlight to −180°C in shadow — the most extreme temperature range of any planet, yet ice may hide in permanently shadowed polar craters.

Only two spacecraft have visited: Mariner 10 in the 1970s and MESSENGER, which orbited from 2011 to 2015 and mapped the whole strange, iron-rich little world.
