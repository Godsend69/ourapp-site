HISTORY

Saturn is a gas giant formed over 4.5 billion years ago, the second largest planet — so light for its size it would float in water if an ocean big enough existed.

Its glorious rings are made of countless chunks of ice and rock, from dust grains to house-sized boulders, spanning 280,000 km yet often only about 10 meters thick.

Galileo first saw the rings in 1610 but his telescope was too weak to resolve them; he thought Saturn had “ears.” Huygens explained them as a ring in 1659.

Its giant moon Titan has a thick atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane, while tiny Enceladus jets water into space from a hidden ocean — both prime places to search for life.
