HISTORY

The Moon was born about 4.51 billion years ago, most likely when a Mars-sized world called Theia struck the young Earth and the debris flung into orbit gathered into our companion.

It is locked in step with Earth, always showing us the same face — the near side — while the far side stayed hidden until spacecraft first photographed it in 1959.

Its surface is a record of violence and time: dark plains of ancient hardened lava called maria, and bright highlands saturated with craters from billions of years of impacts.

In 1969 it became the only other world humans have walked upon. Its gravity raises Earth’s tides and slowly steadies our planet’s spin — a quiet guardian of the rhythm of life.
