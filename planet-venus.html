HISTORY

Venus is nearly Earth’s size and was born from the same early material, yet it became the hottest planet — hotter even than Mercury — due to a runaway greenhouse effect.

A thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide traps heat so completely that lead would melt on its surface, hidden beneath sulfuric-acid clouds that never part.

Venus spins backward and astonishingly slowly: a single day on Venus lasts longer than its entire year.

As the brightest object in our sky after the Sun and Moon, Venus has guided humans as the “morning star” and “evening star” since the dawn of astronomy.
