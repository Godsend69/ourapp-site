HISTORY

Mars formed about 4.5 billion years ago. Its rusty color comes from iron oxide — literal rust — covering its surface in fine red dust.

Billions of years ago Mars was warmer and wetter, with rivers, lakes, and possibly a northern ocean. Dry riverbeds and mineral deposits left by water are still visible today.

As its core cooled, Mars lost most of its magnetic field, and the solar wind stripped away its thick atmosphere, leaving the thin, cold world we see now.

Mars hosts Olympus Mons, the tallest known volcano in the solar system, and Valles Marineris, a canyon system stretching over 4,000 km. It is the most explored planet beyond Earth.
